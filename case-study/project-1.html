Emobodied Virtual Agents for
Improving Social Interactions

Adolescents experiencing extended medical isolation often face severe loneliness, depression, and significant barriers to maintaining social connections with their peers. This multi-phase research project focused on bridging that gap by designing and evaluating embodied conversational virtual agents in a VR environment to mediate and ease social interactions.

Validated design guidelines for social mediation through embodied agents

The Challenge

Medically isolated teenagers face unique vulnerabilities, making direct user research challenging. The core challenge was to design a socially mediating VR experience that felt natural, safe, and engaging, without causing additional emotional strain on an already highly vulnerable population.

Human-Centered Design Methodology

To protect the target demographic from unnecessary emotional vulnerability during the initial discovery phase, I utilised a proxy-research approach before moving into co-design with a comparable age group and testing with them.

Phase 01
Expert Insights

Conducted surveys and in-depth qualitative interviews with healthcare professionals; including oncology nurses and social workers to understand the realities of medical isolation from a systemic perspective.

Phase 02
Co-Design

Facilitated co-design sessions with a group of adolescents without lived experiences of medical conditions to design the characteristics of the embodied virtual agent character as social mechanics.

Phase 03
Evaluation

UX and Usability evaluation sessions with adoelscents with lived experience of medical isolation to investigate the impact of embodied virtual agents on social interactions in collaborative virtual environments.

My Role as a UX Designer & Researcher

Design Approach Execution Details
Approach 01
Familiarity First
 As a researcher with an age gap and language/cultural background difference, bonding with participants was key. I prioritised interactive ice-breaking activities to establish a comfortable, safe environment for teenagers to share openly.
Approach 02
Brainstorming
 Conducted diverse ideation activities: 8-minute rapid-writing for CVC roles, sticky-notes to capture liked/disliked behaviours, and sketching exercises to help participants visualize and express design concepts.
Approach 03
Strategic Discovery
 Looked beyond surface-level feature requests to uncover underlying requirements driving user behavior. Translated raw participant suggestions into core user needs and virtual agent behaviours.
Approach 04
Prioritisation
 Managed design scope using a structured framework: adolescent dot voting to evaluate user desirability, followed by an Effort vs. Impact Matrix to assess development feasibility.

Technical Implementation & VR Development

Following the co-design phase, I built a fully functional, multiplayer VR prototype across three iterative development cycles as the solo developer. The environment was designed to feel refreshing and expansive, actively counteracting the confinement of long-term hospitalisation.

Analytical Rigor

To determine if the embodied virtual agents actually improved social interactions, the final prototype was tested in comprehensive user studies with adolescents with/without lived experience of medical isolation. A partially Nested Mixed Factorial study design was used.

Isolating Experimental Condition

Because social sessions involve multiple people, isolating the true effect of the embodied virtual agent required advanced statistical modeling.

Linear Mixed-Effects

Conducted Linear Mixed-Effects modeling analysis using R to account for variables and isolate the virtual mediator's impact on social behavior.

Group Dynamics

Developed sociograms and applied K-means cluster analysis to map and deeply understand diverse user interaction patterns.

Triangulation

Complemented statistical analysis with rigorous thematic analysis of post-study qualitative data for a holistic view.
