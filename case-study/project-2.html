HCI Research · Empathic Design · MR/VR Dev

SeeThrough: Bridging the Empathy
& Expertise Gap

Transforming static visual impairment goggles into a dynamic Mixed Reality simulation tool. Designed to foster real empathy among architects and urban designers, helping them understand the lived experience and the “WHY” behind design accessibility codes like NZS 4121:2001.

Role
Lead Researcher & Developer
Key Skills
Empathic Design · XR Development · Workflow Integration · Prototyping
Tech Stack
Unity 3D, Shader Graphs, URP, Mixed Reality, Virtual Reality
Outcome
Demonstrated increased empathy and comprehension of accessibility codes among architecture students
Mixed Reality (MR) Virtual Reality (VR) Empathic Design Unity 3D Shader Graphs Accessibility

The "Compliance vs. Reality" Gap

Architects and planners frequently follow accessibility building codes as a compliance exercise, lacking a true understanding of the lived experience of vision impairment.

  • Invisible Barriers: Design professionals often lack the firsthand perspective needed to see hazards before they are built.
  • Costly Mistakes: Uninformed design decisions create permanent physical barriers that are highly expensive to retrofit once a building is finished.
  • Static Limitations: Traditional fixed goggles cannot adapt to new digital designs or represent the complexity and variability of different vision conditions.
  • Expert Shortage: A lack of qualified personnel to assess every project means accessibility is too often treated as an afterthought.

Discovery & Empathic Design

Given a strict three-month timeline to research, design, and develop a solution, the focus was prioritised heavily on driving empathy for design decision-makers rather than building a flawless medical diagnostic tool.

Requirements Gathering
Stakeholder Alignment

Conducted initial meetings to define the core requirement: delivering a tech solution specifically for the professionals involved in urban design and built environment decision-making.

Lived Experience
Partnering with Blind Low Vision NZ

Collaborated for contextual understanding and experience sharing, gathering grounded requirements to ensure the product fostered genuine empathy.

Prototyping & UX
Customisable Overlays & Workflows

Designed low-fidelity interfaces to map different visual impairment profiles (e.g., Glaucoma, AMD) and integrated "Workflow Presets" so designers could save and re-test profiles across project stages.

Technical Implementation & XR Development

The simulation was built using Unity 3D, relying heavily on the Universal Render Pipeline (URP) and Shader Graphs to create highly customisable visual impairment parameters that respond in real-time.

Feature / Stack Implementation Details
Shader Graph Overlays
 Customised shaders using variables for severity, blur, distortion, and colour parameters. Utilised available medical resources to stylise these overlays for empathic accuracy.
Impairment Simulation
 Programmed logic to simulate colour blindness (blue, red, green, and full) and spatial blind spots (central blind spot, curtain blind, peripheral loss, and multiple blind spots).
MR Auditing (See-Through)
 A pass-through mode allowing professionals to physically walk through existing spaces with digital overlays to identify real-world hazards.
VR Pre-Visualisation
 A mode for testing digital architectural designs proactively before a single brick is laid, enabling barrier-free decision-making early in the process.

Outcomes & Impact

The prototype was evaluated with architecture students to measure its effectiveness in bridging the empathy gap. The results demonstrated a clear shift in perspective: participants were able to accurately explain the "WHY" behind specific accessibility design guidelines.

Crucially, by experiencing these simulated visual impairments firsthand, the students showed a profound increase in empathy for the Blind and Low Vision (BLV) community, clearly articulating the severe real-world challenges that arise when inclusive design guidelines are not implemented.
